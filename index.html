Ginger

Ginger (Zingiber officinale), is a rhizome plant that is very popular as a spice and medicinal ingredient. The rhizome is finger-shaped which bulges in the middle segments. Ginger thrives at an altitude of 0 to 1500 meters above sea level, except for elephant ginger at an altitude of 500 to 950 meters. To be able to produce optimally, it takes 2500 to 3000 mm of rainfall per year, 80% humidity and moist soil with a pH of 5.5 to 7.0 and high Nutrients. The soil used for planting ginger should not be flooded.

Gambir

Gambir is a type of dried sap derived from the crushed extract of the leaves and twigs of the plant of the same name. Indonesia is a supplier of 80% of gambier commodities in the world market because it has the best quality. The process goes through six stages, namely picking leaves, boiling leaves and twigs, pressing, settling, draining, printing and drying.
